What this is

Not a course.
Not a workshop.
A remembering.

Each session we come together as six women and we remember. Not who we are supposed to be. Not the daughter, the sister, the wife, the mother, the colleague. All of that gets left at the door.

For 90 minutes every Sunday you are just you. Fully, completely, without apology.

We open with a guided visualisation — not a relaxation exercise, but a doorway. A way of accessing the deepest parts of yourself that the thinking mind cannot reach alone. What surfaces there is real. Then you write it. Unedited, unfiltered, no stopping. This is not journalling as habit. This is journalling as excavation.

Then we talk. Live coaching that witnesses you, challenges you gently, and holds you accountable to what you actually want. Because the women in this room are not just participants. They are mirrors. Each one reflecting something back to you that you could not see alone.

This is a space where you take responsibility for what you want to create. Where you remember that you are not waiting for life to happen. You are already co-creating it, right here, right now. Together.

You leave every session with a recording to return to. Life happens — if you ever need to miss a live session, the recording means you never lose the thread. You leave the six weeks with something more valuable than that — a group of women who have watched you come home to yourself, and who you have watched come home too.

This is not about fixing yourself. You were never broken. This is about remembering.

Every session. Every week.

The journey

Six weeks.
One arc.

Each week builds on the last. You arrive not knowing where you'll end up. That's the point.

01

Permission

We begin here. A guided visualisation opens the space — and then you write. No performance, no editing, no right answers. You arrive exactly as you are, and that is enough.

02

Excavation

Who were you before the world got loud? We go beneath the roles, the habits, the identity built for survival. Then you write what surfaces. She's still in there.

03

Name It

The things you've stopped saying. The specific ways you have silenced yourself — in your relationships, your work, your own head. This week you write them down. Without shame.

04

Dissolve

The identity that no longer serves you gets examined and gently released. What are you carrying that was never yours? What are you still doing out of habit, fear, or the need for approval?

05

Rewrite

This is where it gets electric. You stop planning to live as the new version and start actually doing it. You write her. You speak her out loud. You are witnessed doing it. The old story loses its grip.

06

Rebirth

You share. We witness. We celebrate. You leave this room knowing who you are, what you want to say, and that you are not alone in becoming her. This is the beginning, not the end.

The story

I've spent my life in the study of being human. On stage, on screen, on the page, and in the room with people who are trying to figure out who they are.

I trained as an actor, which taught me how to disappear into other people with extraordinary precision. In theatre and in film. It took me years to understand I had been doing the same thing off set and off stage. Performing a version of myself that worked, that was legible, that kept everyone comfortable. Until it didn't work anymore.

The process of dismantling that — of asking who I actually was beneath the performance — is what became Words & The Work.

I know what it is to be articulate about everyone else's experience and completely mute about your own.

I started Unplug With Ani as a podcast — conversations with entrepreneurs and creatives about their journeys. Over time, I realised the most important conversation I needed to be having was the inward one. The podcast became a space for that. This container is the live version.

I'm a published poet, my debut collection HOPE is available on Amazon. I'm a working actor in theatre and film. I'm a qualified life coach. But more than any of that: I am someone who has done the messy, necessary, joyful work of figuring out who she actually is and I want to hold that space for you.

What this is

The invisible things
are the ones that run everything.

Most people spend their lives working on what they can see — the behaviour, the pattern, the symptom. Very few go to where it actually began.

This work does.

In a 1-1 space with me, you go underneath. Underneath the story you have been telling yourself. Underneath the conditioning you absorbed before you had the language to name it. Underneath the version of yourself you built to survive — and toward the one that was always there waiting.

This is not talking about your life. This is excavating it. Using guided visualisation, deep reflective work, and live presence to access the parts of you that thinking alone cannot reach.

I don't fix you. I witness you. And in being truly witnessed — something shifts that years of trying to figure it out alone never could.

I don't just hear what you are saying. I see what is beneath it. That has always been my gift — long before it became my work.

Who this is for

Open to men and women
ready to do the real work.

This work is for anyone — man or woman — who is ready to stop managing the surface and start understanding what is underneath it.

  • You have achieved things by most definitions — and still feel something essential is missing
  • You carry patterns from your upbringing that you can see clearly but cannot seem to break
  • You want to understand yourself at a level that changes not just what you do but who you are
  • You are navigating a transition — in identity, relationship, career, or sense of self
  • You feel things deeply and have spent a long time not knowing what to do with that
  • You are ready to be honest — with yourself, and in the room

How I work

Guide. Witness.
Space holder.

I have been holding space for people my entire life — long before I formalised it as work. As an actor, I learned how to inhabit the truth of human experience. As a poet, I learned how to find the precise language for things that resist being named. As a facilitator, I learned how to create the conditions for real things to surface safely.

I hold qualifications in counselling (NCFE Levels 1-3) and life coaching (Advanced Diploma, Achology Certified) — not as titles, but as foundations. What I bring into the room goes beyond any qualification. It is the capacity to see the soul of a person and reflect it back to them with clarity, compassion, and zero judgement.

Sessions draw on guided visualisation, deep reflective dialogue, somatic awareness, and writing as an excavation tool. Each one is entirely led by what you bring and what the work calls for. No two sessions look the same.

Guide
I don't tell you who to be. I help you find your own way back.
Witness

Space Holder
I create the conditions for real things to surface. Then hold them with you.
